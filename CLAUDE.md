# Project Guidelines — projects.derekurban.dev

## Purpose

This is Derek Urban's **project gallery** — a curated, visually-driven showcase of work across university, personal, and professional projects. It is not a blog. It is a living portfolio meant to document, showcase, and provide technical depth on things built throughout a career as a developer, creator, and builder.

## Core Values

### Gallery First
Every decision should serve the gallery experience. Projects are the stars — the site exists to frame them well. Navigation, layout, and interaction patterns should make browsing feel effortless and visually rewarding.

### Show, Don't Tell
Favor visuals, demos, and artifacts over walls of text. A screenshot, a diagram, or an embedded demo says more than a paragraph. Written context supports the visuals, not the other way around.

### Substance Over Flash
Design should be polished and intentional, but never at the expense of clarity or content. Every visual choice should have a reason. Decoration without purpose is clutter.

### Honest Representation
Present work accurately. Credit collaborators. Be transparent about project scope, my role, and the state of completion. This is a portfolio, not marketing material.

## Organization Principles

### Tag-Based, Not Category-Based
Projects are organized through a flexible tagging system rather than fixed categories. A project can be simultaneously tagged as "University", "Python", "Machine Learning", and "Complete". This enables versatile filtering and cross-cutting discovery.

### Tag Dimensions
Tags should cover multiple dimensions of a project:
- **Origin** — Where the project came from (University, Personal, Professional)
- **Domain** — What area it belongs to (Web, Hardware, Design, Data, etc.)
- **Technology** — What tools and languages were used
- **Status** — Where the project stands (Complete, In Progress, Archived)

### Every Project Tells a Story
Each gallery entry should convey: what was built, why it was built, what was learned, and what the outcome was. The depth of that story can vary — some entries are quick showcases, others are deep dives — but the intent is always present.

## Content Principles

### Breadth of Work
This gallery spans software and non-software projects. It reflects the full range of building, creating, and problem-solving — not just code.

### Technical Depth is Optional but Welcome
Not every visitor wants implementation details. Surface the highlights first, and let curious readers dig deeper. Layer information so the casual browser and the technical reviewer both find value.

### Living Portfolio
This site grows over time. New projects get added. Old entries can be updated with retrospective insights. The structure should accommodate growth without reorganization.
