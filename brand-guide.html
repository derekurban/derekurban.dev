Strategic Italic Usage
Create something beautifully ugly

The soft editorial feel of Newsreader should be used as a contrast device inside a headline, not as the default voice of the entire system. It works best when it punctuates one emotionally resonant phrase and lets the surrounding text stay grounded and legible.

Use italics when
  • Emphasizing a short phrase inside a display headline.
  • Adding softness, elegance, or a sense of contrast to a hero statement.
  • Calling attention to a poetic, human phrase that benefits from visual lift.
  • Using <em> for semantic emphasis in hero and manifesto-style copy.
Avoid italics when
  • Setting full paragraphs, UI labels, or dense explanatory copy.
  • Turning entire headlines italic unless the moment is intentionally editorial.
  • Using multiple italic phrases in one headline and diluting the contrast.
  • Relying on italics alone for hierarchy when size, spacing, or color should do the job.

03 â€” Logo

The Derek Urban wordmark

Newsreader set in title case for Derek Urban. The wordmark should feel understated, personal, and editorial rather than startup-branded.

Derek Urban On light
Derek Urban On dark
Derek Urban On accent
Derek Urban On white
Use title case
The name should appear as Derek Urban. It reads like a personal byline, not a stylized startup mark.
Keep it understated
Let type, spacing, and restraint carry the personality. The wordmark does not need symbols, flourishes, or extra ornament.
Avoid all caps
Do not render the name as DEREK URBAN in logo contexts. It becomes harder and less personal than the rest of the system.
Do not distort the wordmark
Maintain the natural proportions of Newsreader. No condensing, expanding, or skewing the name.
